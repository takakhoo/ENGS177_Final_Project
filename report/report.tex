\begin{abstract}\noindent
We model dynamic stock-bond allocation as a Partially Observable Markov Decision Process (POMDP) whose latent state is the prevailing macro-financial regime. A two-state Gaussian Hidden Markov Model (HMM) is fit by Baum--Welch on the VIX and the 10Y--3M Treasury term spread, and a Bayesian filter maintains a belief over the latent regime. The associated fully-observable Markov Decision Process (MDP) is solved by both Value Iteration and exact Policy Iteration (cross-checked to agree within $2.6\times 10^{-6}$), and a QMDP approximation \cite{littman1995} is applied to the belief state at each rebalance. On a 2003--2026 backtest of SPY and AGG with monthly rebalancing and 5\,bps transaction costs, we run an eleven-baseline horse race spanning the academic and practitioner consensus (static 60/40, equal-weight, inverse-vol, risk-parity ERC, Markowitz with Ledoit--Wolf shrinkage, Faber 10-month SMA, time-series momentum, vol-targeting, HMM-conditional mean-variance, Black--Litterman with HMM-derived views, and myopic 12-month). In the original two-channel fixed-HMM configuration, QMDP at CRRA $\gamma{=}2$ finishes \emph{last} of twelve on Sharpe (0.73 vs.\ Faber's 1.68 and the static 0.81), reflecting an ``all-stocks'' collapse of the underlying MDP. Two diagnostic experiments \emph{reverse} this verdict in isolation: (i)~adding the Chicago Fed NFCI and St.\ Louis Fed STLFSI4 as observation channels produces a regime-differentiated policy (100/0 bull, 0/100 bear) at the same $\gamma{=}2$; (ii)~switching from fixed to expanding-window walk-forward refit \cite{nystrup2018} lifts QMDP Sharpe to 1.08 and cuts max drawdown from $-34\%$ to $-16\%$. The negative headline is therefore a diagnostic of methodology, not a fundamental verdict; we close with a Powell~\cite{powell2019}-style positioning of QMDP as a value-function approximation with one-step direct lookahead, and three extensions.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}\label{sec:intro}
%================================================

A long-only investor must choose, every month, the weights of a portfolio of two assets: a U.S.\ equity index (SPY) and a U.S.\ aggregate-bond index (AGG). The textbook 60/40 split is a \emph{static} allocation that implicitly assumes the joint return distribution is stable. Markets violate this in two specific ways: returns are conditionally heteroskedastic (calm decades broken by short crises), and stress periods exhibit higher cross-asset correlations so bond diversification erodes when it is most needed. In 2022 a 60/40 portfolio lost roughly 17\% as both legs sold off under persistent inflation, motivating tactical regime-aware overlays.

\paragraph{Research question.} We test the implicit claim of every regime-switching paper: \emph{Can a fully decision-theoretic regime overlay---formalised as a POMDP, fit to public macro-financial data, solved by a tractable belief-state heuristic---beat the static $60/40$ benchmark out-of-sample after realistic transaction costs?} By ``decision-theoretic'' we mean the allocation is the solution of an explicit optimisation, the latent regime is treated as a hidden state inferred by a Bayesian filter, and the policy is the QMDP approximation of an underlying POMDP. Three observations together force the POMDP formulation: the regime is latent, the observations are noisy, and decisions are sequential. Decision trees explode with horizon; influence diagrams blow up the same way for repeated rebalances; fully-observable MDPs assume what we cannot observe.

\paragraph{Contributions.} (i)~an eleven-baseline horse race spanning the academic and practitioner consensus (\Cref{sec:horserace}); (ii)~a multi-feature observation-cohort study (\Cref{sec:multifeature}) showing that adding financial-stress indicators \emph{fixes} the QMDP policy collapse; (iii)~a walk-forward refit study (\Cref{sec:walk-forward}) showing that the Nystrup et al.\ \cite{nystrup2018} protocol lifts QMDP Sharpe to 1.08; (iv)~a Powell-taxonomy positioning of QMDP as a VFA + one-step DLA; (v)~a reproducible public repository \texttt{takakhoo/ENGS177\_Final\_Project} with 14 data series, 12 strategies, and 12 experiment scripts.

\section{Background}\label{sec:background}
%================================================

\paragraph{Regime switching in finance.} The Markov-switching model is due to Hamilton \cite{hamilton1989} who applied a two-state model to U.S.\ GDP. Ang and Bekaert \cite{ang2002} demonstrated that ignoring regimes produces welfare losses in international allocation, but their gain mechanism is concentrated in the cash-switching margin---``costs of ignoring regimes are small for all-equity portfolios.'' Guidolin and Timmermann \cite{guidolin2007} formalised multi-asset allocation under a 4-state HMM and reported certainty-equivalent gains of 1.5--3.0\% per annum. Nystrup et al.\ \cite{nystrup2018} address the look-ahead-bias problem via online walk-forward refit. Tu \cite{tu2010} examines whether regime switching matters once model, parameter, and regime uncertainty are jointly accounted for; the answer is qualified, and the parameter region in which the regime label does \emph{not} move the policy (low risk aversion, two risky assets) is exactly where our negative headline lives.

\paragraph{POMDP framework.} A Partially Observable MDP \cite{kaelbling1998} assumes the agent observes only a noisy function of the latent state and maintains a probability distribution (belief) over the state. Exact POMDP solution is PSPACE-hard; the QMDP approximation \cite{littman1995} projects the belief onto the action-value function of the fully-observable MDP. We use QMDP because (i)~it is exact when the belief is a Dirac measure, (ii)~it reduces to a standard infinite-horizon MDP solver, and (iii)~in our setting there is no action that improves observability of the regime (we observe VIX and the term spread regardless of allocation), so the well-known weakness of QMDP---failure to motivate information-gathering actions \cite{hauskrecht2000}---does not bind. Point-based value iteration \cite{pineau2003} is a tighter alternative but unnecessary on our 2-state model with a 1-dimensional belief simplex.

\paragraph{Powell taxonomy positioning.} Powell \cite{powell2019} unifies sequential decision-making into four meta-classes: policy function approximations (PFAs), cost function approximations (CFAs), value function approximations (VFAs), and direct lookahead approximations (DLAs). QMDP fits naturally as a \emph{VFA} in which the belief-state value function $V(\bel)$ is approximated by the linear lower envelope $V_{\mathrm{QMDP}}(\bel) = \max_a \sum_s b(s) Q^\ast_{\mathrm{MDP}}(s,a)$, combined with a \emph{one-step DLA} at decision time. This sentence is the project's theoretical anchor: we are using the MDP value function as a VFA on the belief MDP and selecting actions via a one-step lookahead.

\paragraph{Practitioner consensus.} The empirical winner of published two-asset tactical horse races (Faber \cite{faber2007}, Hurst--Ooi--Pedersen ``Century of Evidence'' \cite{hurst2017}, Moskowitz--Ooi--Pedersen \cite{moskowitz2012}, Moreira--Muir \cite{moreira2017}) is consistently \emph{time-series momentum with volatility targeting and cash fallback}, reporting Sharpe 0.85--1.10 on SPY+AGG 1990--2024. This is the empirical bar our QMDP must clear.

\subsection{POMDP formulation}\label{sec:pomdp}

\begin{definition}[POMDP]\label{def:pomdp}
A POMDP is the tuple $(\mathcal{T},\state,\action,T,\Obs,O,R,\lambda)$ where $\mathcal{T}=\{0,1,\dots\}$ is the set of monthly epochs; $\state=\{s_1,\dots,s_K\}$ is the finite set of latent regimes; $\action=\{\mathbf{a}_1,\dots,\mathbf{a}_M\}$ is the discrete grid of long-only stock-bond weights; $T(s'\mid s)$ is the HMM transition matrix (action-independent because regimes are exogenous to portfolio choice); $\Obs=\R^d$; $O(\mathbf{o}\mid s)=\mathcal{N}(\mathbf{o};\boldsymbol{\mu}_s,\boldsymbol{\Sigma}_s)$ is the Gaussian emission; $R(s,\mathbf{a})=\E_{\mathbf{r}\mid s}[U(\mathbf{a}^\top\mathbf{r})]-c\lVert\mathbf{a}-\mathbf{a}_{\rm prev}\rVert_1$ is the single-period reward with CRRA utility $U$ and $c=5$\,bps; $\lambda=0.95$.
\end{definition}

\paragraph{Belief filter.} Given previous belief $\bel_{t-1}$ and observation $\mathbf{o}_t$, the Bayes update is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:bayes}
\bel_t(s') = \frac{O(\mathbf{o}_t\mid s')\sum_s T(s'\mid s)\,\bel_{t-1}(s)}{\sum_{s''}O(\mathbf{o}_t\mid s'')\sum_s T(s''\mid s)\,\bel_{t-1}(s)}.
\end{equation}
Initialise $\bel_0$ at the stationary distribution of $T$.

\subsection{HMM calibration via Baum--Welch}\label{sec:hmm}

The Gaussian HMM joint density factors as $p(\mathbf{o}_{1:T},s_{1:T})=\pi_{s_1}O(\mathbf{o}_1\mid s_1)\prod_{t=2}^T T(s_t\mid s_{t-1})O(\mathbf{o}_t\mid s_t)$. Baum--Welch is the EM algorithm tailored to this factorisation: the E-step computes forward $\alpha_t(s)=p(\mathbf{o}_{1:t},s_t{=}s)$ and backward $\beta_t(s)=p(\mathbf{o}_{t+1:T}\mid s_t{=}s)$ messages; the smoothed posterior is $\gamma_t(s)=\alpha_t(s)\beta_t(s)/\sum_s \alpha_t(s)\beta_t(s)$; the M-step updates parameters by weighted MLEs \cite{rabiner1989}. We use \texttt{hmmlearn.GaussianHMM} with \texttt{covariance\_type='full'}, five random restarts, tolerance $10^{-4}$. Model selection across $K\in\{2,3,4\}$ uses BIC: $K{=}2$ is preferred at 271 monthly observations on $d{=}2$ channels. Fitted parameters on 2003--2014: bull (mean SPY $+1.16\%$/mo, VIX 15.2, 87 mo); bear (mean SPY $-0.14\%$/mo, VIX 27.7, 48 mo). The stationary distribution is $(0.60,0.40)$.

\subsection{MDP solvers and QMDP}\label{sec:vi-pi}

The underlying MDP's optimal value function satisfies the Bellman equation $V^\ast(s)=\max_a\{R(s,a)+\lambda\sum_{s'}T(s'\mid s)V^\ast(s')\}$. Value iteration applies $V^{(n+1)}=LV^{(n)}$ and stops at $\|V^{(n+1)}-V^{(n)}\|_\infty<\varepsilon(1-\lambda)/(2\lambda)=2.6\times 10^{-6}$ for $\lambda{=}0.95,\varepsilon{=}10^{-4}$, converging in 125 iterations. Policy iteration solves the linear system $(\mathbf{I}-\lambda\mathbf{P}_\pi)V=\mathbf{r}_\pi$, then greedy-improves; converges in 2 iterations.

\begin{proposition}[Solver agreement]\label{prop:agreement}
On our calibrated MDP with $\lambda{=}0.95$, $\gamma{=}2$, VI and PI produce identical greedy policies and value functions agreeing within $2.6\times 10^{-6}$ component-wise, the empirical realisation of the contraction-mapping equivalence~\cite{puterman}.
\end{proposition}

The QMDP rule \cite{littman1995} is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:qmdp}
\pi_{\mathrm{QMDP}}(\bel)=\argmax_{\mathbf{a}\in\action}\sum_{s\in\state}\bel(s)\,Q^\ast(s,\mathbf{a}),
\end{equation}
with $Q^\ast(s,\mathbf{a})=R(s,\mathbf{a})+\lambda\sum_{s'}T(s'\mid s)V^\ast(s')$.

\subsection{Backtest protocol and metrics}\label{sec:protocol}

Walk-forward over 271 monthly observations 2003-10-31 to 2026-04-30. Monthly rebalancing. Transaction cost $5\times 10^{-4}\,\lVert w_t-w_{t-1}\rVert_1$ per period. Each policy initialised at the stationary regime distribution and $(0.6,0.4)$ weights. Performance metrics (in \texttt{src/utils/metrics.py}): CAGR, annualised volatility and downside vol, Sharpe and Sortino ratios, Omega ratio, max drawdown, Calmar, Ulcer index, tail ratio, hit rate, average $\ell_1$ turnover, and information ratio vs.\ benchmark.

\section{Discussion and Conclusions}\label{sec:conclusions}
%================================================

The narrative that emerges from \Cref{sec:horserace,sec:gamma-sweep,sec:multifeature,sec:walk-forward,sec:subperiod} is this: our headline ``QMDP at $\gamma{=}2$ underperforms 60/40'' is correct under the specific joint configuration of (a)~narrow VIX+spread observations, (b)~fixed in-sample HMM, (c)~low CRRA. Each component is empirically reversible---adding NFCI and STLFSI4 differentiates the regime policy at the same $\gamma{=}2$; expanding-window refit lifts Sharpe by 33\% and halves the drawdown; raising $\gamma$ to 8 shifts the policy bondward and crosses the static benchmark. The negative finding is a diagnostic of methodology, not a fundamental verdict on POMDP asset allocation.

Our findings align tightly with the literature: Ang and Bekaert's \cite{ang2002} ``costs of ignoring regimes are small for all-equity portfolios'' predicts the headline (two-risky-asset universe, no risk-free leg); Guidolin and Timmermann \cite{guidolin2007} predict the multi-feature unlock (richer observations or more states are needed to differentiate regime policies); Nystrup et al.\ \cite{nystrup2018} predict the walk-forward unlock (regime persistence beats the inference latency under expanding-window refit). What is unique to our work is the joint diagnostic decomposition---publishing a negative result with explicit decomposition of \emph{which} methodological component drives the verdict, all positioned within Powell's \cite{powell2019} four-class taxonomy (QMDP = VFA + one-step DLA).

\paragraph{Three extensions.} \emph{(i)~CVaR-penalised reward.} Replace CRRA with $R(s,\mathbf{a})=\E[\mathbf{a}^\top\mathbf{r}\mid s]-\kappa\mathrm{CVaR}_\alpha[\mathbf{a}^\top\mathbf{r}\mid s]$ to explicitly penalise bear-regime tail risk; one-line change in \texttt{src/utils/utility.py}. \emph{(ii)~Point-based VI \cite{pineau2003}.} Maintain explicit $\alpha$-vectors over a small set of reachable beliefs. On a 1-D belief simplex the cost is negligible and provides a tight upper bound on QMDP. \emph{(iii)~Off-policy MC evaluation via per-decision importance sampling \cite{precup2000}.} Lets one set of trajectories evaluate every candidate policy in parallel; pairs naturally with a Double Q-learning model-free baseline \cite{vanhasselt2010}.

\paragraph{Honest verdict.} The practitioner consensus winner for two-asset tactical allocation---time-series momentum with vol-targeting and cash fallback, approximated here by Faber's 10-month SMA---remains best on our 2003--2026 sample. Our QMDP, in its best-configured variant (expanding-window walk-forward, richer observations, CRRA $\gamma\geq 5$), is \emph{competitive} but does not dominate. We do not claim a Sharpe win for POMDP asset allocation. We do claim a correctly-positioned POMDP framework (VFA + DLA in Powell's taxonomy) is the right \emph{minimum} tool for the decision problem, and that the headline negative finding is a diagnostic of methodology rather than fundamentals.
